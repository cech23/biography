BIOGRAPHY

Biography of Ann Antw​i (Aka Akua Sekyeiwaa)

Ann Antwi (Akua Sekyeiwaa) was born on the 21st of July 1982 at Formena Government Hospital to the late Chief Inspector Francis Appiah Antwi, a Police Officer, and Deaconess Esther Boadi-Mensah also of blessed memory, civil servant with the Birth and Death Registry.  Ann grew up in a loving and a very happy home which she shared with her five other siblings in a tightly knit family with strict Christian values; values which shaped her through her adult life.  Ann began her formal education at the Akomadan, where our late father was stationed as a police officer and later to Mante-Din and Oninku JSS in Tema, and sailed through the basic level successfully. Ann continued her secondary education at African Advance College,in Ashiman, where she studied Business and Secretarial Courses. It was at African Adavance College that Ann met friends like Beatrice (Bee) and Lizbeth(Alocious) which friendship later blossom to sisterhood. She completed the three-year program in the with flying colors. While awaiting the release of her final results, after her secondary SSSCE Examination,  Ann developed the desire to learn something with her hands and trained herself in the art of dress making.   Ann had a bubbly, outgoing, engaging personality and great fun to have around you. She was also a great conversationalist and was always looking for avenues to enhance her life. This quest coupled with her outgoing nature eventually made her rediscover her culinary skills.  Ann eventually became a caterer as she kept receiving orders to provide friends and clients some of her delicious dishes at events such as engagements, weddings, parties and other social events, to the admiration of those who engaged her services. Ann would once again, owing to her many social engagements as a caterer, would rediscover another hidden talent or gift of MC-ings. Endowed with great oratory skills, which gift she offered as her service to God when she played the role of an interpreter at her Church, Calvery Divine Church- Sakumono, would soon blossom into a budding vocation. Ann’s gift of choosing the right words which Mcing events and her diction was widely acknowledged and admired by many patrons which earned her nicknames like “Musical interlude”, “Akua Blessing” and “Akua Donkor” in various quarters. In 2004, Ann married Mr. Asamoah Amponsah, a man she happily shared her life with for some years, which union resulted in the birth of her only child, Master Kwabena Ohene Amponsah, who is currently in SSS2 in Prempeh College-Kumasi.  Ann will be sorely missed by all who encountered her during her bubbly and illustrious lifetime.  May her good soul rest in perfect peace! 

Tribute from Siblings

God looked around His garden and found an empty place. He looked down upon the earth and saw someone He truly loves. He put his arms around you and lifted you to rest, but you did not go alone for part of us went with you!

 We still can’t fathom why the almighty chose to call you so early away from us. We have still not recovered from the shock, which the news of your sudden death sent our spines. We are hanging on to the Serenity prayer which says “God, give us the strength to accept what cannot be changed, the courage to change that which could be change and the wisdom to know the difference”. Ann, we wish you were still here with us to enjoy life, the plan to get you your own restaurant, but God loves you more and wants you by His side.  We remember, with nostalgia, as children games we played together, laughter we had together, songs we sang together, fights we had and yet remained brothers and sisters.  You played a motherly role after the demise of our dear mother, taking care of Akofa, a role you played with lots of love and care until your untimely departure. All the memories we shared, the secrets we shared, we grew up to find we have a love for each other that is very special and very strong.  You are our sister not by choice but by nature of our birth. We could not have chosen a much better sister; you are the best on earth, and this is true!  Life indeed, as the old adage says, is full of uncertainties; today you are here, tomorrow you are gone. The mortality of men is a certainty, which as humans we tend to lose sight of until we are called one day to be with our maker.  Ann Antwi, da yie. Demerifa due.  You will be missed  by all. We know you are in the bossom of the lord.  Rest in peace!

TRIBUTE BY THE SON

Kwabena Ohene Ampomah

A mother's love is a light to every child's soul and to have a Godly caring mother is a blessing. Mama Akua, on that black Wednesday morning when preparing for vacation classes I told Dady that grandma died early at the age of 67 that grandma should have waited for me to grow to take care of her, little did I know that you will soo soon depart at such young age. I called you that black Wednesday morning to check on you, you told me you were not feeling well which I advised you go to hospital, again little did I know that was going to be the last time I will hear or see you  My mother was lovely and caring that every child would have loved to have as mother, she gave me life, fed me, clothed me, thought me  shouted at me when the need arose. An inspirational mother who always advice’s  me to be respectful to all manner of persons, take my education serious as that will be my perfect future picture and not to depart from my creator. I asked myself what will life be to me in this wicked world without a mother, but I take comfort that ‘’it is appointed unto man to die and after death judgment” (Hebrews 9:27), comfort that I have I have Auntie Maggie, Dady Ohene's wife, my lovely Aunties, lovely Mothers and other love ones  Mama Akua, I love you, let your spirit continue to watch over me and protect me till we meet again  Rest Well, Rest Well, Rest Well
